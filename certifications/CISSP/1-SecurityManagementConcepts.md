# ISC2 CISSP: Security Management Concepts

## Code of Ethics

Ethic is set of moral principles, or values that we live by
Principles for profession conduct

ISC2 expects to uphold Code of Ethics

Required to fully commit to fully support this code of ethics
Violations subject to action
Obligated to report

Canons
* Protects society, the common good, necessary public trust, and the infra
    * Safeguard info and systems that are essential to public's safety and welfare
    * Consider the societal impact of actions and decisions
    * Do your best to protect or and customer info
    * Thoroughly analyze security needs of anything you are responsible for
    * Provide or recommend the necessary security to protect all people and assets
* Act honorably, honestly, justly, responsibility, and legally
    * Maintain personal integrity and honesty in all professional dealings
    * Be clear and transparency w/ colleagues, client and stakeholders about security capabilities and limitations
    * Be ethical in approach
    * lead by example
    * If you make mistake be accountable
* Provide diligent and competent service to principals
    * Maintain high level of professional competence and stay current with technological developments
    * Avoid conflicts of interest and deliver reliable service
    * Perform high quality work and service
    * Serve your employers to the best of your abilities
    * Improve and grow security skillset
* Advance and protect the profession
    * COntribute to the growth and maturity of practices
    * Share knowledge, mentor others and participate in professional orgs
    * grow and share your knowledge and sills
    * keep up on current threats and attacks

Canons in practice
* Protect everything you are responsible for
* Act with good intentions
* Provide you best efforts
* Advance knowledge in skills

Ethical conduct ensures professionals to take responsibility for their actions

In practice
* Promote a culture of ethical behavior
* regularly reflect on how decision align

Understand the importance of integrity and accountability in ethical decision making

## Information Security Concepts

Confidential: ensure data is not disclosed to an unauthz to person process
Integrity: ensure data is not altered or changed by unauthz person/process
Availability: ensure data is available for use by authz person/process

Data is not disclosed, altered or deleted

CIA Triad applies everywhere

Authentic: Data or actions coming from legitimate subject
Authenticated: Subjects must prove to be authentic through ID and authentication (log in)

Non-repudiation: Proof that the data or actions originated from a subject an cannot be disputed (log in is not enough)

completed by Used cryptography (Digital signature)
